how to fall in love

a short guide
words:
shannon russell
& nick adams

First you need to meet, but you can't know it. It has to be late at night — dark — so you can only see each other's silhouettes. There has to be other people around who keep you at a distance, creating an unknowable barrier that can only fall with time.

Then, time. You need to forget it all. You need to meet again and not remember the first time. You need to laugh and drink tea and climb mounds of dirt and look at each other as the lights of the city twinkle in the reflection of your eyes and not know an ounce of the joy life has in store for you.

You need to be apart; you need to be together. You need to travel great distances in little time and then go nowhere for days. You need to cook for each other, sing to each other, dance with each other. You need to have nostaligia for the nights of creaky hardwoord floors and too much red wine. You need the one thing that kept you apart in the beginning to become the thing that makes two lives mix slowly into one: time. The unstoppable force rivalled only by the love it has created for you. The refrain between the introductions. The gift you will share together for the rest of your life.

All you need, is time.
